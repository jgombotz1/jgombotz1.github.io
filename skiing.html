Skiing at Heavenly Mountain the morning after 2 feet of snow had fallen. The majority of the mountain was closed, but we found a way to get to areas of consistently untracked powder. One of the most beautiful days I can remember.

Yet another beautiful day at Heavenly Mountain. There had been hours of dense fog that day, but in a matter of minutes it lifted, allowing the sun to break through and creating an ethereal looking scene.

Photoshopped? Not quite. My extremely talented snowboarding friend Dawson was able to get enough speed to flip over my friends. It's amazing how much height you can get with a little help from a jump and a snowmobile.

Will taking a quick break from skiing in one of our favorite areas at Heavenly Mountain. The Burn is an expanse of charred trees, left from years ago when a fire burned through the area.

During this morning, I was waiting in the lift line for the mountain to open. We watched the fog as it rolled up the valley, and soon there was enough mist where we were standing to allow the sun's rays to show beautifully through it.

On Christmas Day last year, my family was skiing at Stevens Pass, Washington. Riding up the lift together, we were amazed to see this rainbow appear on the ridge. At first we all thought we were imagining it. A Christmas miracle? I'd say so.

This photo is from the Burn at Heavenly Mountain. As the sun was breaking through the clouds, it hit this tree perfectly to make it look like it was glowing. I'm so glad I was able to capture this moment.

My father has always been my greatest skiing inspiration. You can see him here ripping between these trees in Steamboat, Colorado. This mountain was absolutely stunning, and the sky was the deepest blue I have ever seen as you can see in this photo.
