This is a watercolor painting of South Lake Tahoe during the Summer time. The color of the water here is always so strikingly blue, it was a fun challenge to capture it's vibrant colors.

This is the first oil painting I ever completed. Before this, I had no idea I was capable of painting anything that looked remotely realistic, but with the help of Bob Ross, I was able to prove myself wrong.

This is an oil painting of the view from my cabin in Leavenworth, Washington. During the winter here, the sunsets often transform the mountains and sky to shades of pink and purple.

This oil painting is meant to be of Mt. Rainier, but I ended up making the peak much more jagged than it is in reality. Even so, I still loved the way the reflection in the water turned out.

This acrylic painting was inspired by one of my favorite Pacific North West artists; Mimi Kvinge. Like her, I draw the majority of my inspiration from the Mountains.

This acrylic painting is my favorite piece I have completed so far. Unlike any of my other paintings, I did not plan it out at all. Instead, I began by painting a gradient, which later I decided to paint the silhouette of a tree over. Soon after, I found myself adding texture and color to the tree, which transformed it into something I am very proud of.
